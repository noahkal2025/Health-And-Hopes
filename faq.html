Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our medical tourism services

General Questions

Medical tourism is when patients travel to another country to receive medical care. This is often done to access specialized treatments not available locally, benefit from lower costs, or receive care from world-renowned specialists. Health & Hopes Medicare International facilitates this entire process for Ugandan patients.

We work with top-tier hospitals in India, USA, Germany, Canada, UK, Italy, Israel, South Africa, Egypt, and Kenya. India is a popular destination for its cost-effectiveness, but we provide financial assessment to help determine the most suitable destination based on your medical needs and financial capability.

Simply contact us via phone, email, or WhatsApp. Our team will schedule a consultation to understand your medical needs, review your medical reports, and recommend the best hospitals and specialists for your condition. We'll guide you through every step of the process.

Yes, when done through a reputable agency like Health & Hopes Medicare International. We only partner with internationally accredited hospitals that meet strict quality and safety standards. All our partner hospitals have experienced, board-certified specialists and state-of-the-art facilities.

Billing & Account

Our consultation and coordination services are provided at competitive rates. The total cost depends on the specific services you need (visa assistance, travel arrangements, hospital coordination, etc.). We provide transparent pricing and detailed cost estimates before you commit to anything.

We accept various payment methods including bank transfers, mobile money, and cash payments at our office. For hospital payments abroad, we can guide you on the best methods including wire transfers and international payment services.

Yes, absolutely. We work with hospitals to provide detailed cost estimates for your treatment, including surgery, hospital stay, medications, and follow-up care. This helps you plan your budget accurately before making any travel arrangements.

Coverage varies by insurance provider and policy. Some international insurance plans do cover medical tourism. We can provide all necessary documentation for insurance claims, including detailed invoices and medical reports. We recommend checking with your insurance provider before proceeding.

Technical Support

Medical visa processing typically takes 3-7 business days once all documents are submitted. However, we work to expedite the process when urgent medical care is needed. We handle all documentation and liaise directly with embassies to ensure fast-track processing.

You'll need a valid passport, medical reports and scans, a referral letter from your local doctor, hospital invitation letter (which we help obtain), passport photos, and proof of financial capability. We provide a complete checklist and help you organize all required documents.

Yes, we arrange medical attendant visas for up to two family members or caregivers to accompany you. We also help with their travel arrangements and accommodation near the hospital.

We provide comprehensive post-treatment support including follow-up coordination with your treating doctors abroad, liaison with local healthcare providers in Uganda, and assistance with obtaining and managing your medical records. Our support continues even after you return home.

Yes, we have expedited processes for urgent medical cases. Our team works around the clock to arrange fast-track visa processing, immediate hospital appointments, and urgent travel arrangements when time is critical.
